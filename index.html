Diversity in Tech

Article written to display on Wonder Women Tech's website

Wonder Women Tech (WWT) understands that the world is changing right before our very eyes, which is why we have made it our duty to do something about it. Now more than ever before, we must uplift and promote discussion about hidden opportunities for diversity in technology. We need more women, more people of color, more people with disabilities, more people in the LGBTQA community to become the innovators of the world we live in. We don’t live in a world that’s black and white, and we never have. Our world is painted in a million vibrant colors, and we each happen to be the paint brushes. In this day and age, we are no longer the “minority” because there is nothing minor about us. Our population is growing at rapid speeds, yet we still are underrepresented in so many areas. So the question we want to ask is, why is the majority being underrepresented and why haven’t we done anything about it? We must be the change we expect to see, and it starts here at Wonder Women Tech.

A Look Back in Time…

Movements and marches are opportunities to show the world what a group of people can do when they stand together for a cause. If someone is discriminated against because they don’t fit into what some believe are societal norms, you must do something about it. In school, we are encouraged to break the silence about bullying. In the real world, we should follow that same concept. Without protests, we would live every day of our lives abiding to irrationality, segregation and inequality.

CIVIL RIGHTS MOVEMENTS

The Civil Rights movement was a series of protests to end racial segregation and discrimination towards African-Americans. For nearly 100 years after the abolishment of slavery, African Americans were still treated unequally and denied full civil rights. Jim Crow laws restricted them from attending many public schools, restrooms, restaurants, theaters, and other public places. The modern civil rights movement began after an African American woman by the name of Rosa Parks was arrested for refusing to sit in the back of the bus. During its most prominent years from the 1950s to 1960s, leaders like Martin Luther King Jr., Booker T. Washington, Rosa Parks, Andrew Goodman, and thousands of blacks and whites protested peacefully in Washington D.C. against inequality. Because of this movement, Congress passed the Civil Rights Act of 1964 and the Voting Rights Act of 1965, which ended segregation and removed discriminatory obstacles that prevented non-whites from voting.

WOMEN’S RIGHTS MOVEMENTS

The Women’s Rights movement fought for equality and voting rights for women. Women were treated as less than men and were not allowed to go to college, pursue professions in medicine or law, be a part of juries, and participate in the affairs of the church. In addition, married women were not allowed to own property and husbands had legal power over their wives and children. Men were favored in every instance, whether it was in the court of law or at the work place. The first Women’s Rights Convention was held in Seneca Falls, New York, where 68 women and 32 men joined together to outline the Women’s Rights movement. They later held a national convention that attracted more than 1,000 participants. Elizabeth Cady Stanton, Susan B. Anthony, Matilda Joslyn Gage, Lucy Stone, and Sojourner Truth were the women that pioneered this movement. They continued to hold conventions around the country to lecture and address these gender biases. After 72 years of campaigning, women were granted the right to vote under the 19th Amendment.

LGBT EQUAL RIGHTS AND LIBERATION MARCH

The LGBT Equal Rights and Liberation March gathered approximately one-million supporters protesting against lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender discrimination and violent oppression. Those who were openly gay were not allowed to serve in the military and ROTC programs. As a cause of this, 13,000 people were expelled from the U.S. Armed Forces. Members of the LGBT community were also discriminated against when trying to adopt children and during custody battles. The rapid spread of AIDS also became a huge concern to the LGBT community. Marchers came up with a list of demands to end discrimination in society towards homosexuals, as well as secure government funding for AIDS research. Although the march only lasted one day, the fight for equality has never stopped. As of today, homosexuals can adopt children, get married in all 50 states, and best of all, express themselves much more openly.

These movements and marches have done a substantial amount of progress, but we are still not in the environment to create an equitable, thriving world. We must continue where these historical figures left off. They all had a reason worth fighting for, and so do we. It’s our turn to make a difference and make things right.

Our Solution

We need to hold discussions about increasing diversity and inclusion in technology over the next decade. We need to ask ourselves what are the local solutions we need to build today to amplify Silicon Valley’s values of diversity and inclusion. We must figure out how we can continue to hold the region and companies accountable to hire women and embrace diversity. As we have seen in history, unity and discussion are the answer to bringing everyone together.

Be a part of the mission

We are changemakers. At Wonder Women Tech, we are at the brink of change, and seizing this opportunity. We are making a difference about difference.

To continue this discussion, join us at the Evening with Wonder Women Tech on March 1, 2017 or live stream the event at WonderWomenTech.com

Inns of the Corps Grand Opening

Article written to display on MCCS Okinawa website

Inns of the Corps paves the way for a memorable “home away from home” experience, allowing us to take exceptional care of Marines and their families through hospitality.

After three years of planning, design and construction, MCCS Okinawa was proud to introduce the Inns of the Corps Camp Hansen at a grand-opening ceremony on Monday, August 19. This brand-new, $14.8 million facility (construction of which was 100 percent locally funded) is the first execution of the newly designed MCCS Inns of the Corps brand standards on Okinawa. This new approach aspires to transform Temporary Lodging Facilities, or TLFs, by providing high levels of style, comfort, well-being and peace-of-mind to intransit Marines and their families during their permanent change of station or temporary assignment of duty.

MCCS & MCIPAC Assistant Chief of Staff Ed Hutsell, MCCS Lodging Program Manager Sid Hattangadi, Camp Hansen Camp Commander Colonel Larry Jenkins and MCIPAC Commanding General Brigadier General William Bowers performed the ceremonial ribbon-cutting to officially welcome the public to this new and vastly improved lodging facility on Camp Hansen that promises to deliver wonderful experiences for all who stay there.

Inns of the Corps Camp Hansen has a total of 36 rooms, with 26 standard rooms and 10 extended stay suites. All standard rooms come with two queensized beds, a refrigerator and a microwave. For those looking to stay a bit longer, our extended stay suites come with a private bedroom with a queensized bed, living room with a pullout sofa and a full kitchen and dining area, perfect for a nice dinner with the whole family. And because we wish to take care of the whole family, including your furry friends, we offer two pet-friendly rooms—one standard and one extended stay suite. And lastly, to wind down after a long day of work, guests can enjoy a moment of peace and tranquility at the Inns of the Corps Camp Hansen Japanese Garden, only steps away from their rooms.

To make your reservation today, please call 098-969-4511 or visit mccsokinawa.com/hansen_lodge for more details.

Data Privacy: How to Protect Your Online Information

Article written to display on Wonder Women Tech's website

Data Privacy Day (DPD) is celebrated every year on January 28. Although DPD is over, we should continue practicing Data Privacy every day of the year. The purpose of DPD is to bring awareness to online privacy and the protection of your personal information. DPD began in the United States and Canada back in 2008, and is now celebrated globally. Millions of people are digitally connected, but that also raises many concerns for online information theft. Here are some tips to help you stay protected:

Be Up-to-Date with Updates

When speaking of data privacy, updates don’t commonly come to mind, but in fact they are quite important. Regularly update your web browser and operating system to ensure virus, malware and other online protection for your computer.

Update Your Operating System:
  • Windows
  • Mac
Update Your Web Browser:
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Google Chrome
  • Internet Explorer
  • Microsoft Edge

Enable Privacy Options for your Email

Your sent and received emails are full of confidential information, especially when using a business email account. Emails are one of the most popular platforms for malware infections and spam. Make sure to enable privacy features to help protect your email messages.

Manage your Email Privacy Settings:
  • Gmail
  • Yahoo
  • Outlook

Protect your Social Presence

Let’s face it, we want our private lives to become the next public sensation, but sharing too much can potentially be dangerous. Protect your social media platforms by changing your security and privacy settings.

Manage your Social Media Privacy Settings:
  • Facebook
  • Instagram
  • Twitter
  • Pinterest
  • LinkedIn
  • Tumblr

Avoid Sensitive Information on Public Wi-Fi

The Wi-Fi network we’re using isn’t really one of our concerns, as long as the Wi-Fi works, right? Let’s reconsider that thought. Public Wi-Fi and other shared internet networks can give people the ability to access to your personal information. This doesn’t mean you shouldn’t use public Wi-Fi, this just means, be more selective about what you do on public Wi-Fi. Next time you go to your local coffee shop, the library or the airport, avoid checking your online banking or other sensitive information that requires a username and password. Also, never trust hotspots run by people you don’t know, this may also cause information theft. Your best bet is to use password protected internet networks.

Data Privacy is a serious matter. You should be the only one who has full control of your personal information on the World Wide Web. Don’t make Data Privacy Day the only day you protect your online information. Protect your information all year long!
